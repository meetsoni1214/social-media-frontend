# 2-Week MVP GTM Experiment (India)

Dates: Thursday, February 5, 2026 to Wednesday, February 18, 2026

## Assumptions

- One vertical chosen for launch.
- MVP supports creating and publishing at least 4 posts in 7 days.
- English + 1 regional language.
- Basic outcomes tracked (posts, profile views, inquiries, clicks).

## Experiment Goal

Validate demand and retention by proving 7-day ROI for one vertical:
“Businesses can publish 4–6 branded posts in a week and get measurable visibility or inquiries.”

## Success Metrics (Minimum Viable)

- 40–60 qualified leads contacted
- 12–15 signups
- 8 onboarded (complete profile + publish 1 post)
- 4 retain through Day 7 (publish 4+ posts)
- 2 report a concrete outcome (inquiries, reach, profile views)

## Week 1 — Build and Launch (Feb 5–Feb 11)

### Feb 5 (Day 1) — Choose ICP + Offer

- Pick one vertical and one city tier (e.g., salons in Tier‑2 cities).
- Define the 7‑day outcome statement.
- Create a 1‑page pitch and a simple signup form.

### Feb 6 (Day 2) — Content and Template Pack

- Build 8–12 templates tailored to the vertical.
- Include festival/local moments relevant in Feb.
- Prepare 2 “proof” examples (before/after brand colors).

### Feb 7 (Day 3) — Landing Page + Onboarding

- Add a landing page with 3 elements:
  - Outcome promise
  - 2 screenshots
  - 3‑step onboarding
- Add a light compliance note and consent for data use.

### Feb 8 (Day 4) — Lead List

- Build a list of 60–80 local businesses for outreach.
- Sources: Instagram, Google Maps, JustDial, local WhatsApp groups.

### Feb 9 (Day 5) — Outreach Wave 1

- 30 direct DMs or calls.
- Pitch: “We’ll create and schedule 4 posts for you in 7 days for free.”

### Feb 10 (Day 6) — Onboard First Cohort

- Get 5–7 businesses onboarded.
- Ensure they publish 1 post in first 24 hours.

### Feb 11 (Day 7) — Feedback Loop

- Collect friction points.
- Identify which template types drive the fastest publish.

## Week 2 — Validate and Iterate (Feb 12–Feb 18)

### Feb 12 (Day 8) — Improve Onboarding

- Remove top 2 onboarding blockers.
- Add one success prompt: “Post 2 more this week.”

### Feb 13 (Day 9) — Outreach Wave 2

- 30–40 more leads using refined pitch.
- Focus on businesses similar to the most active users.

### Feb 14 (Day 10) — Proof of Value

- Capture 2 short case examples:
  - Before/after profile look
  - Change in profile views or inquiries

### Feb 15 (Day 11) — Retention Push

- Message all users with a 7‑day challenge:
  “Post 4 times this week, we’ll deliver 2 bonus creatives.”

### Feb 16 (Day 12) — Pricing Signal Test

- Ask for willingness to pay:
  - “Would you pay monthly if we deliver X posts + scheduling?”
  - Use a simple form.

### Feb 17 (Day 13) — Conversion Offer

- Offer a limited “early access plan” to 2–3 best users.

### Feb 18 (Day 14) — Decision Gate

- Decide: double down, pivot vertical, or pause.

## What to Track Daily

- New leads contacted
- Signups
- Onboarding completion
- Posts published
- Outcomes reported
- Drop‑off reasons

## Decision Gate

Proceed only if you hit at least:

- 8 onboarded
- 4 active (4 posts in 7 days)
- 2 with measurable outcome

If not, pivot:

- Change vertical
- Change outcome promise
- Increase automation
